A quick note on HSL (hue, saturation, lightness) colors:

Hue is measured on a circle (0-360 degrees) that looks like this:

0red
60yellow
120green
180cyan
240blue
300magenta

Saturation is measured on a scale from 0-100, where 0 is completely unsaturated (i.e. gray) and 100 is completely saturated (i.e., the richest, least gray, possible color).

Lightness is measured on a scale from 0-100, where 0 is black, 100 is white, 50 is right in between. If you have a saturated color (say, red), then "lighter" means "more pink", and "darker" means "more maroon". (Try it!)

Your standard, fully-saturated colors will have a saturation value of 100, and a lightness value of 50.
